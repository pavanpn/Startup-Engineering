The cost and physical size of camera has been dramatically reduced over the past years. However current surveillance technologies are still far from utilizing these advantages. The sensing devices also are capable of doing much more algorithms and hence are much more intelligent.

'Intelligent Collaborative Surveillance' makes use of multiple surveillance cameras which work in collaborative manner and is more reliable against attacks, cost less overall, need less energy to run and provides much more analytics leading to less dependency on human.

Our software has two modules. The first module helps takes provides motion detection and tracking functionality based on concept ofcamera switching. Both wired and wireless cameras can be used depending on the surveillance area. The second module provides face detection and recognition functionality in surveillance video.

We have a working prototype of our software but we still have approximately two months of development and testing for which we are require funds. This includes UI/ UX design, addition of security features and testing on multiple hardware configurations. We are committed towards releasing the product in time.

FAQ

What is the scope of this system?
With rapid urbanization, increase in population and increase in crimes, need for more sophisticated surveillance systems are becoming essential. Monitoring the video monitors of the surveillance site by people is not very reliable as it may involve negligence.

The problem addressed by the system is the redundancy that is inherent in most of the present video surveillance systems which rely on centralized monitoring of video streams, wherein each camera feed is treated as an isolated stream. We have implemented a new surveillance strategy and its implementation, in which multiple cameras work in a collaborative manner in order to reduce the number of effective video streams.
Where can this system be implemented?
Our model of surveillance builds on this principle of distributed intelligence at the edge i.e. sensors. It can be implemented right from small offices to airports. Instead of tens of camera large camera our system will embeds hundreds of CMOS cameras each with their own battery backup and each with WiFi network. These cameras may be embedded inside wall of the walk ways and rooms, waiting areas and may be made visible to alert common people that the areas are under surveillance. However many of these cameras will be dummy and does not yield any data making it difficult for attacker to seek cover or attack the sensors.

Our model of collaboration is to enable exchange of events as sensed by each of the sensors to the other sensors around which helps the next sensors to decide its activity. For example, if an chaos is detected by one sensors, it may alert all other sensors to get active and start streaming. As it is clear, till this time, a good chunk of the sensors might be doing nothing.

The scope of collaboration will be determined by the surveillance goal. For example, in a ratial environment, the goal will to detect shop-lifting and for Airport the focus is to detect terrorist trying to smuggle arms or drugs.
